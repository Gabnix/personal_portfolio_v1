# Frontend Design Context: The Architect Portfolio

> Design context for Jim Ling's personal portfolio. Read this before making any design decisions.

---

## 1. Required Context

### Target Audience
Senior Engineering Managers, technical recruiters, and startup founders at top-tier tech companies (FAANG, high-growth startups).

They are:
- Time-constrained  
- Pattern-matching quickly  
- Holding very high visual and technical standards  

They will leave within ~90 seconds unless something earns their attention.

---

### Primary Use Cases
- Reviewing technical project deep-dives (especially **SoundSmiles**)  
- Scanning professional experience and skills  
- Quickly locating GitHub / LinkedIn / contact pathways  

---

### Primary Conversion Goal
> Get users to open the **SoundSmiles case study**, then book an intro call.

Every layout, interaction, and visual decision must reduce friction toward this path.

---

### Brand Personality
**Refined Engineering — Technical. Sophisticated. Impactful.**

- Feels like a premium physical object  
- Precise, intentional, and highly considered  
- Not “creative for the sake of it”  
- Signals competence, clarity, and craftsmanship  
- Warm, but never casual or sloppy  

---

## 2. Design Direction: "The Architect"

### Purpose
A professional platform to showcase high-quality software engineering work with extreme clarity, precision, and accessibility.

---

### Tone
**Luxury / Refined Minimalist / Editorial**

Move away from:
- “Hacker” dark modes  
- Generic SaaS templates  

Move toward:
- **Paper & Ink (Light)**  
- **Deep Obsidian (Dark)**  

With obsessive attention to whitespace and hierarchy.

---

### Core Differentiation

#### The "Type-First" Hero
- No headshot  
- Massive, beautifully kerned typographic introduction  
- Fluid scaling using `clamp()`  
- Typography carries the first impression entirely  

---

### Constraints
- Must use **Tailwind CSS** with **OKLCH colour functions**
- Strict adherence to **WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility**
- **Mobile-first**, using **Container Queries**
- Full parity between **light and dark modes**

---

## 3. Design Principles

### 1. Restraint Amplifies Signal
- Whitespace is a feature, not empty space  
- Remove before adding  
- Every element must justify its existence  

---

### 2. Typography IS the UI
- Hierarchy comes from:
  - Size  
  - Weight  
  - Spacing  
  - Line height  
- No decorative UI to compensate for weak type
- Headings should feel inevitable  
- Body text should feel effortless  

---

### 3. The Accent is Sacred
- Use **one accent colour only**  
- Only for:
  - Primary CTAs  
  - Key links  
  - Conversion-driving elements  

**Never** use accent colour:
- Decoratively  
- In backgrounds  
- For visual noise  

---

### 4. Motion Reveals Engineering
- Animations must feel:
  - Physically correct  
  - Intentional  
  - Precise  
  - Use: cubic-bezier(0.19, 1, 0.22, 1)
- No bouncy or playful motion  
- Respect reduced motion preferences  

---

### 5. Convert, Then Impress
- The site exists to drive:
  1. SoundSmiles case study views  
  2. Intro call bookings  

If a design decision does not support this → remove it.

---

## 4. Frontend Aesthetics Guidelines

### Typography

**Display Font**
- High-contrast serif (e.g. Playfair Display, Fraunces, Young Serif)  
- Communicates a thoughtful, architectural tone  

**Body Font**
- Clean neo-grotesque (e.g. Geist Sans, Instrument Sans, Inter Tight)  
- Base size: `1.125rem`  

**System**
- Use a **Major Third typographic scale**  
- Avoid system defaults  
- Typography must feel intentional and engineered  

---

### Colour & Theme

**Technique**
- Use **OKLCH** for perceptual consistency  

---

#### Base System
- Fully **achromatic foundation** (greyscale)  
- Ensures focus, professionalism, and clarity  

---

#### Dark Mode Palette
- **Surface**: `oklch(18% 0.01 260)`  
- **Text**: `oklch(92% 0.01 260)`  
- Avoid pure black/white  

---

#### Accent (Choose ONE)
- **Deep Emerald**: `oklch(0.52 0.17 162)`  
- **Vibrant Indigo**: `oklch(0.55 0.22 275)`  

Used sparingly and only for conversion direction.

---

### Layout & Space

**Composition**
- Asymmetric **12-column grid**  
- Leave first **2–3 columns empty** on large screens  
→ Creates editorial sidebar feel  

---

**Spacing Rhythm**
- Tight grouping for related information  
- Large separation (**12rem+**) between major sections  
→ Content must breathe  

---

**Structure**
- Avoid heavy “card-based” layouts  
- Prefer:
  - Subtle dividers  
  - Typographic section headers  

---

## 5. Interaction & UX

### Micro-interactions
- Staggered reveal on scroll  
- Use `IntersectionObserver`  

---

### Motion Feel
- Weighty, controlled, engineered  
- Never playful or decorative  

---

### UX Requirements
- Optimistic UI for contact actions  
- Clear, frictionless navigation to:
  - SoundSmiles  
  - Contact / booking  

---

### Accessibility
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliant  
- Strong `:focus-visible` states  
- Keyboard navigation must feel first-class  

---

> If it looks like a SaaS template, delete it.

---

### Goal
The site should feel:
- Hand-crafted  
- Engineered with care
