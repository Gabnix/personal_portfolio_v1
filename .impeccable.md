# Frontend Design Context: The Architect Portfolio (v2.0)

> Refined for Sticky-Nav & Interactive Minimalism. Based on Brittany Chiang benchmarks.

---

## 1. Required Context

### Target Audience
Senior Engineering Managers, technical recruiters, and startup founders at top-tier tech companies (FAANG, high-growth startups).

They are:
- Time-constrained  
- Pattern-matching quickly  
- Holding very high visual and technical standards  

They will leave within ~90 seconds unless something earns their attention.

---

### Primary Use Cases 
- Scanning professional experience and skills  
- Quickly locating GitHub / LinkedIn / contact pathways  

---

### Primary Conversion Goal
> Get users to open the **SoundSmiles case study**, then book an intro call.

Every layout, interaction, and visual decision must reduce friction toward this path.

---

### Brand Personality
**Refined Engineering — Technical. Sophisticated. Impactful.**

- Feels like a premium physical object  
- Precise, intentional, and highly considered  
- Not “creative for the sake of it”  
- Signals competence, clarity, and craftsmanship  
- Warm, but never casual or sloppy  

---

## 2. Design Direction: "The Modern Architect"

### Tone & Style
**Hyper-Minimalist / Engineering-First / Interactive**
- **Architecture**: Two-column layout on desktop.
- **Left Column**: Fixed/Sticky branding and navigation.
- **Right Column**: Scrollable content stream.

### Constraints
- **Tailwind CSS** with **OKLCH** color functions.
- **WCAG 2.1 AA** accessibility.
- Mobile-first (single column) transitions.

---

## 3. Design Principles

### 1. Structural Persistence
The Left Column (Name, Title, Nav) must remain static. This provides a constant anchor for the brand while the right-side technical details flow.

### 2. Interaction as Information
- Use a **Mouse-Following Spotlight** effect (radial gradient) to add depth.
- Navigation links must reflect the current scroll position (Scroll-Spy).
- Hover states should signal "engineered precision" (subtle shifts, no bounce).

### 3. Glass & Refraction
Unlike standard SaaS templates, use **Glassmorphism** for structural utility:
- Backdrop blurs for fixed navigation headers.
- Transparent cards that inherit the background spotlight.

---

## 4. Frontend Aesthetics Guidelines

### Typography
**Unified Neo-Grotesque**
- **Fonts**: Inter Tight or Geist Sans.
- **Display**: Massive intro text using `clamp()`.
- **Metadata**: Overlines in `text-[12px]` with `0.18em` tracking.
- **Body**: Base size `1.125rem` with `leading-relaxed`.

### Colour & Theme (OKLCH)
- **Foundation**: Achromatic (Greyscale).
- **Dark Mode Surface**: `oklch(18% 0.01 260)`
- **Accent (Vibrant Indigo)**: `oklch(0.55 0.22 275)` — Use strictly for key CTAs and "Active" states.

### Layout & Spacing
- **Desktop**: Split 40/60 or 33/66 (Left/Right).
- **Gutter**: Consistent `px-6` to `px-12` across all breakpoints.
- **Vertical Rhythm**: Generous padding (`py-24`) between major sections.

---

## 5. Specific Component Logic

### The Navigation
- Vertically stacked links in the fixed column.
- Active section indicator: A horizontal line that extends (`w-16`) on hover or active scroll.

### The Project/Experience Cards
- Avoid heavy borders.
- Use `hover:bg-slate-800/50` or `hover:shadow-[inset_0_1px_0_0_rgba(148,163,184,0.1)]`.
- Tech stack items should be small, high-contrast pills.

---

## 6. The AI Slop Test
- **Avoid**: Pure black backgrounds, generic icon kits, and standard Bootstrap-style cards.
- **Goal**: The site must feel like a high-performance single-page application, not a multi-page document.
